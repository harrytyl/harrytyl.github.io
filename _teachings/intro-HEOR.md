## Course Overview

This course is designed for students who aspire a career in health sciences in general, and in health outcomes research in particular. This course delves deeper into the principles of outcomes research, with a major emphasis on research methodology. It is expected that the students have received enough exposure to the principles of study design, epidemiological methods, and basic understanding of statistical analysis of the data. This course is aimed at enhancing such knowledge through a deeper understanding of research methodology. A major emphasis of the course is on hands-on experience with data analysis.

## Audience

This course is intended for 4th year undergraduate students.

## Learning Objectives

- To describe foundations of health outcomes research and epidemiological research designs used in pharmacy research
- Interpret and apply common biostatistical analytic techniques, and assess their appropriate applications
- Obtain hands-on, practical experience with data analysis for health outcomes research, and to perform basic quantitative data analyses on a given dataset including descriptive statistics
- Identify threats to the validity of empirical studies
- Evaluate published research to make judgements about the validity, value, and applicability of the findings to patient care
- Recognize principles of ethics in research and become familiar in processes of obtaining ethical approval for research studies
